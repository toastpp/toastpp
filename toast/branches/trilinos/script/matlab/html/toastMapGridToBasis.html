toastMapGridToBasis

Map an image between two different basis representations: from g-basis to b-basis (see Basis definitions).

Syntax

bimg = toastMapGridToBasis (hBasis, gimg)

Parameters

[In] hBasishandlebasis mapper handle
gimgreal or complex [g]image (basis coefficients) in g-basis expansion
[Out] bimgreal or complex [b]image (basis coefficients) mapped into b-basis expansion
Dimensions: g = size of g-basis, b = size of b-basis.

Notes

Arbitrary functions can generally not be represented exactly in any of the basis expansions. Therefore, mapping from one basis to another may incur a loss, and mapping back to the first basis may not recover the original basis coefficients precisely.

Images can be real or complex-valued.

This function is similar to toastMapGridToSol, but it maps the complete bounding box of the problem domain, including voxels outside the area of support. This is useful e.g. for displaying images.

Both g and b-basis are regular grids. The mapping between them is implemented as a bi- or tri-linear interpolation.

If the hBasis handle was created by a call to toastSetBasis with only a single regular grid parameter, then the g-basis and b-basis expansions are identical, and toastMapGridToBasis passes the input image directly through to output.
