toastMapGridToSol

Map an image between two different basis representations: from g-basis to s-basis (see Basis definitions).

Syntax

simg = toastMapGridToSol (hBasis, gimg)

Parameters

[In] hBasishandlebasis mapper handle
gimgreal or complex [g]image (basis coefficients) in g-basis expansion
[Out] simgreal or complex [s]image (basis coefficients) mapped into s-basis expansion
Dimensions: g = size of g-basis, s = size of s-basis.

Notes

Arbitrary functions can generally not be represented exactly in any of the basis expansions. Therefore, mapping from one basis to another may incur a loss, and mapping back to the first basis may not recover the original basis coefficients precisely.

Images can be real or complex-valued.

This function is similar to toastMapGridToBasis, but in addition it also removes any voxels from the coarser basis expansion that do not overlap with the problem domain. This represents the solution basis of the inverse solver.

Both g and s-basis are regular grids. The mapping between them is implemented as a bi- or tri-linear interpolation.

If the hBasis handle was created by a call to toastSetBasis with only a single regular grid parameter, then toastMapGridToSol is equivalent to toastMapBasisToSol.
